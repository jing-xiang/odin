Pad Thai

Description

This delicious Thai dish is a combination of rice noodles, tofu, eggs, bean sprouts, and peanuts, all stir-fried in a tasty sauce.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces rice noodles
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 block tofu, cut into small cubes
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, chopped
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Cilantro, for serving

Steps

  1. Soak the rice noodles in hot water for 8-10 minutes, or until they are soft.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and shallots and cook for 1-2 minutes, or until fragrant.
  3. Add the tofu to the pan and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes, or until lightly browned.
  4. Push the tofu to one side of the pan and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are cooked, then mix them with the tofu.
  5. Add the rice noodles to the pan and stir-fry everything together for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the fish sauce, soy sauce, tamarind paste, brown sugar, and chili powder to the pan. Stir everything together until the sauce is well combined.
  7. Add the bean sprouts and chopped peanuts to the pan and stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes, or until everything is heated through.
  8. Serve the pad thai with lime wedges and cilantro.
